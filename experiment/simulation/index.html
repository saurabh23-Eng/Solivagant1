Virtual Lab Experiment: Frequency Shift Keying (FSK)

Objective

To study and simulate the working principle of Frequency Shift Keying (FSK) modulation using a virtual interactive waveform display.

Theory

Frequency Shift Keying (FSK) is a digital modulation technique where binary data is represented using different frequencies:

  • Binary 1 → High frequency (f1)
  • Binary 0 → Low frequency (f0)

This is used in radio signals, modems, wireless communications, and IoT devices.

Procedure

  1. Enter a binary input (like 10101) into the field below.
  2. Click on "Generate FSK Signal".
  3. Observe the frequency changes on the waveform plot below.
Binary 1 = High Frequency Binary 0 = Low Frequency

Result

The waveform displays alternate frequencies for binary input. The high frequency corresponds to binary 1, and low frequency to binary 0.

Conclusion

This simulation helps in understanding how digital bits can be encoded using frequency changes. FSK is reliable for transmitting data over communication channels that require resistance to noise and interference.
